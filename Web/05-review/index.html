Any n × n square matrix A whose elements are in an algebraically closed field K is similar to a Jordan matrix J, also in {\displaystyle \mathbb {M} _{n}(K)}{\mathbb {M}}_{n}(K), which is unique up to a permutation of its diagonal blocks themselves. J is called the Jordan normal form of A and corresponds to a generalization of the diagonalization procedure. [1][2][3] A diagonalizable matrix is similar, in fact, to a special case of Jordan matrix: the matrix whose blocks are all 1 × 1. [4][5][6]
