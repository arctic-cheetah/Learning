What can it do?

Used primarily as a board to interface sensors with the on board computer it can be used to perform a desired output. You can also extend it's capabilities by adding "shields" onto the existing pins of the Arduino and allowing SD card reads, clocks, motor control and many more!

From popular demand, below is a table of the specifications for the entry level Arduino Uno:

Microcontroller ATmega328P
Operating Voltage 5V
Input Voltage (recommended) 7-12V
Input Voltage (limit) 6-20V
Digital I/O Pins 14 (of which 6 provide PWM output)
PWM Digital I/O Pins 6
Analog Input Pins 6
DC Current per I/O Pin 20 mA
DC Current for 3.3V Pin 50 mA
Flash Memory 32 KB (ATmega328P) of which 0.5 KB used by bootloader
SRAM 2 KB (ATmega328P)
EEPROM 1 KB (ATmega328P)
Clock Speed 16 MHz
LED_BUILTIN 13
Length 68.6 mm
Width 53.4 mm
Weight 25 g
